# Candidate Outreach Email (no-questionnaire version)

The questionnaire is a NO-GO, but the outreach email still earns its
send: it is the channel for candidate-supplied headshots, corrections
to the drafted data, and the fairness safety-valve for the stated
positions blocks. One email per campaign; contact pages for all 20
candidates with websites were verified live July 4 (see ballot.json
`website` fields). Send from an editorial address, not a generic one.

---

**Subject: Your entry in the Wausau Pilot & Review voter guide — Aug. 11 primary**

Dear [Candidate name],

Wausau Pilot & Review is publishing a free voter guide for the
August 11 partisan primary, covering every race on ballots in our
area, including yours. Every candidate receives the same treatment:
an identical profile with your name, residence, occupation, a short
background note, a link to your campaign website, and brief "stated
positions" quoted verbatim from your own published campaign materials
with a citation.

Three things before we publish:

1. **Photo.** Please reply with a headshot we have your permission to
   publish (square crop preferred, at least 400×400 pixels). Candidates
   who don't provide one appear with a neutral initials placeholder —
   never a stock image.

2. **Check your information.** Your current entry is attached below.
   If your residence, occupation, or background line is wrong or
   incomplete, reply with corrections. We verify against WEC filings
   and your own published materials.

3. **Your stated positions.** We quote the first issues as ordered on
   your campaign website, verbatim. If your site doesn't state
   positions, your profile shows "No stated positions available." If
   you'd rather we quote a different passage of your own published
   materials, point us to it — we quote exactly, with a citation, and
   we don't accept new statements written just for this guide.

Please reply by **[date — give at least one week]**. The guide
publishes before the July 22 registration deadline.

[Editor name]
Wausau Pilot & Review
[phone / email]

## Send notes for editorial

- **Point 3's "no new statements" rule matters**: rule 8 covers
  *published* campaign materials. A statement written privately for us
  is unverifiable by readers. If a campaign publishes it on their site
  first, we can quote it — that's the workaround to offer.
- Campaigns without websites (Varnam if none is found; any others) get
  the same email via the contact info on their WEC registration.
- Log replies against LAUNCH_CHECKLIST.md §3 and §5. Photos go through
  the PHOTO_SOURCING.md conventions (file naming, photos/README.md).
- No reply = the drafted, editorially verified entry runs as-is with
  the initials placeholder. That is the designed outcome, not a
  failure — don't chase beyond one reminder.
